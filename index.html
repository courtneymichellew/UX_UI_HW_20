Camp Horizon

A non-profit web and mobile redesign.

Project Overview

Camp Horizon helps at-risk, abused and neglected children, most of whom are in foster care, and provides them with a safe place that brings them nourishment, mentoring and love. This organization has a profound impact on the lives of these children and helps empower them to make good decisions to lead healthy and productive lives.

The Problem

Camp Horizon’s current website is in need of an update to enhance its trustworthiness with users who want to donate to the organization.

The Solution

A newly designed user interface including homepage and donation page with convenient checkout modal.

Tools

Figma, Miro, Zoom

My Role

UX and UI Designer (Group project)

Competitor Analysis

During this exercise, we looked at three sites, Camp Twin Lakes, The Boys and Girls Club, and Samaritas. The positive takeaways were to utilize plenty of inviting photography, give a prominent and consistent position for the donation button, and consider a pop-up modal for the donation checkout function.

img

DEFINE

User Persona

Because the themes of the affinity map focused on the experience of a person who wants to donate, we decided that the Donator user persona would be the best one to focus on for the website.

User Insight

A philanthropist who grew up as a foster child now wants to give back by volunteering or donating to an organization that helps foster kids. He discovered Camp Horizon online but finds the website confusing and he fears the organization may be untrustworthy. He worries that the money that he donates won't reach the children that he wants to help. He wishes the website was more clear and concise and the volunteer and donate actions were more easily accessible.

Problem Statement

When researching organizations that help foster children, a potential donor/volunteer finds the Camp Horizon website confusing and he fears the organization may be untrustworthy. He worries that the money that he donates won't reach the children that he wants to help.

How might we redesign the Camp Horizon website to be more intuitive, actionable, and trustworthy for potential donors and volunteers?

IDEATE

User Flow

Next, we created a user flow for the ideal donator’s experience throughout the site so, that we could understand their journey and pain points. Here we decided that the checkout functionality would be housed in a pop-up modal instead of a standard webpage as it is currently. 

The pop-up would help by allowing the user to make a donation from any page on the site. This could also increase the likelihood of someone donating if they can make the donation quickly then continue browsing where they left off. 

Wireframes

Since our focus was on increasing the ability to easily donate, we added an infographic section on the homepage where people could either select an amount to donate or go to the donations page to learn more. We also increased the number of buttons that allowed you to donate through the site.

img

PROTOTYPE & TESTING

Testing

During usability testing, we found that users did not feel that the donation transaction was complete at the end of it so, we increased the size of the confirmation message and added more white space around it to highlight it more.

Style Guide

For the interface, we decided to use the colors in the Camp Horizon logo to stay on brand. We updated the playful, yet hard-to-read font to a more modern and easier-to-read Roboto.
